Rheum - Nashimoto Ui (Meyani / 目やに)

Content Warnings: Self-hatred
Notes: Rheum is a word for mucus especially from the eyes or nose. In this case, it's referring to eye mucus.
  • Japanese
  • Romaji
  • English

古いアルバムを　見つけて開いたの
泣いたりさみしくなったのは
ホコリが目に入るから

写真の向こうはよ　写真の向こうだよ
時流れすでに時まかせ
しなびてもしなびきらず

忘れようとして無駄にして
今更指さし笑われて
知ってか知らずか奴は
ヘラヘラと笑っている

昔　懐かしむ恥さらし
怖いもの見たさで開く
古ぼけて腐るくせに
やたら鮮明な写真

さわりたくもないし
思い出したくもない
近未来　今　見える世界
同じことを言っている

目やにが目やにを笑う

furui arubamu o mitsukete hiraita no
naitari samishiku natta no wa
hokori ga me ni hairu kara

shashin no mukou wa yo shashin no mukou da yo
toki nagare sude ni toki makase
shinabite mo shinabikirazu

wasureyou to shite muda ni shite
imasara yubisashi warawarete
shitte ka shirazu ka yatsu wa
herahera to waratte iru

mukashi natsukashimu hajisarashi
kowai mono mitasa de hiraku
furubokete kusaru kuse ni
yatara senmei na shashin

sawaritaku mo nai shi
omoidashitaku mo nai
kinmirai ima mieru sekai
onaji koto o itte iru

meyani ga meyani o warau

I found and listened to an old album.
I got upset and cried,
but that was really because some dust got into my eye.

What lies on the other side of the photograph? It’s the other side of the photograph, of course.
The flow of time is already up to time itself.
Even while withering, I don’t come to wither.

Trying to forget is futile.
At this stage, just point and laugh at me.
Whether you know it or not, this guy
is cackling like an idiot.

The disgracefulness I yearn for, from the good old days,
I uncover it out of the urge to watch something like a trainwreck.
In spite of the fact that it’s grown old and rotten,
it’s way too vivid, this photograph.

It’s not like I wanna touch it,
and I don’t want to look back on it, either.
In the world I can see in the near future, and in the current moment,
I’m saying the exact same thing.

The pot’s calling the kettle black.1

Translation notes

1 The direct translation of the idiom here from Japanese to English is something like: “the guy with crusty eyes is laughing at someone else’s eye mucus”, which happens to call back to the first line about dust getting in the singer’s eye!
